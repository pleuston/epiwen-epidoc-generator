Epiwen

A research environment for historical inscriptions and archival documents — connecting geographic evidence, material objects, primary sources, facsimiles, source texts, and the prosopographical and bibliographic apparatus around them.

Modules

Geographic
Map

Sites and find-spots mapped on satellite and historical base layers, with overlays from period historical atlases.

 Geographic
Sites

Location and complex records — the spatial and architectural contexts in which document-bearing objects are found.

 Physical
Objects

Material bearers of inscribed or painted texts: walls, pillars, steles, tablets, and other physical supports.

 Primary sources
Inscriptions

Inscribed and carved texts as first-class entities, encoded in EpiDoc/TEI XML with full diplomatic and critical apparatus.

 Documentary
Facsimiles

Rubbings, photographs, and other documentary reproductions; the primary witnessing medium for worn or inaccessible originals.

 Textual
Texts

Canonical, literary, and archival source texts reproduced or quoted in the inscriptions, anchored to reference corpora.

 Prosopographical
Authorities

Authority records for historical persons, institutions, and concepts, with cross-references to VIAF, Wikidata, CBDB, and DILA.

 Scholarly
Bibliography

Bibliographic records for primary editions, archival sources, and modern scholarship, encoded in MODS and searchable by author, title, and year.
