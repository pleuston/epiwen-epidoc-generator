Places

Geographic Authority Sources

Place authority records in this corpus reference the following external authorities.

DILA Place Authority

法鼓山佛教文獻數位典藏 — 地名權威
Buddhist Chinese historical

Controlled vocabulary of Buddhist and historical place names in East Asia, maintained by the Dharma Drum Institute of Language and Culture. Covers monastery sites, mountains, administrative divisions, and pilgrimage routes.

authority.dila.edu.tw/place/

CHGIS — China Historical GIS

中國歷史地理資訊系統（哈佛大學）
Harvard GIS administrative

Harvard University's database of historical administrative units in China, 221 BCE – 1911, with coordinates and temporal extents. The standard reference for historical prefectures, counties, and their changes over time.

chgis.fas.harvard.edu

CBDB — China Biographical Database

中國歷代人物傳記資料庫（哈佛大學）
Harvard biographical place-of-origin

Prosopographical database of Chinese historical figures with indexed place-of-origin and activity locations, linked to CHGIS coordinates. Useful for cross-referencing persons with geographic contexts.

projects.iq.harvard.edu/cbdb

Shanghai Library Open Data

上海圖書館開放數據平台
Shanghai linked data API

Shanghai Library's linked-data platform providing authority records for Chinese geographic names, persons, and organisations, cross-referenced with national standards and international authorities. Offers a REST search API and a SPARQL endpoint for querying place, person, and organisation records as RDF/JSON-LD. Authentication via a free API key (register at the developer portal).

data.library.sh.cn  ·  API docs

Getty TGN

蓋蒂地名辭彙
international hierarchical Getty

The Getty Thesaurus of Geographic Names — a structured vocabulary of geographic names and their relationships, including historical variants. Used in Epiwen for interoperability with Western art-historical and archaeological datasets.

getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/tgn/

Wikidata

維基數據
open linked data QID

Wikidata QIDs serve as a lightweight interoperability anchor for place records, linking to Wikipedia, GeoNames, and other authority systems via wikidata identifiers on place authority records.

wikidata.org
